### 1. Content Marketing (Show, Don't Just Tell)

Number Theory is visually stunning but hard to explain in text. Use the "Visual" part of your app to your advantage.

* **Short-Form Video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts):** Create 30-second clips of the *Sieve of Eratosthenes* or the *Collatz Conjecture* visualization in action. Use trending "educational/satisfying" audio.
* *Hook:* "Did you know prime numbers form this pattern?" or "How RSA encryption actually works."


* **Educational Deep Dives:** Write blog posts or LinkedIn articles about specific concepts (e.g., "Visualizing the Golden Ratio"). Link your app as the "interactive tool" to explore these concepts further.
* **Interactive Snippets:** Share GIFs of your "Glassmorphic" UI on subreddits like `r/math`, `r/educationalapps`, and `r/androiddev`.

### 2. Targeted Community Engagement

Go where math lovers hang out. Do not spam; instead, provide value.

* **Reddit:** Participate in `r/math`, `r/computerscience`, and `r/learnmath`. When someone asks about visualizing primes or understanding Modular Arithmetic, mention your app as a free tool you built to help students.
* **Math Forums & Discord:** Join servers dedicated to competitive math (like AMC/AIME prep) or cryptography. Offer the app as a resource for visualizing complex theorems.
* **Product Hunt:** Launch your app on Product Hunt. The "clean, no-ads, offline-first" aspect appeals heavily to the tech community.

### 3. Leverage "Hyper-Local" Growth (The Language Advantage)

Your app supports **Telugu, Hindi, Tamil, Kannada, and Malayalam**. This is a massive competitive advantage in India.

* **Regional Keywords:** Ensure your Play Store listing uses localized keywords for these languages. People searching for "गणित" (Math) or "பகுப்பாய்வு" (Analysis) should find you.
* **Target Indian Ed-Tech Groups:** Join Facebook and Telegram groups for Indian engineering students (IIT-JEE prep, GATE, or Computer Science students). These students often struggle with Discrete Math/Number Theory.
* **WhatsApp Marketing:** Create a simple, professional "flyer" (image) in regional languages explaining that the app is a free, ad-free tool for students, and share it in educational WhatsApp groups.

### 4. App Store Optimization (ASO) & Retention

If people find your app but don't install it, your listing needs work.

* **Optimize the Title:** Change it from "Number Theory: Visual Explorer" to something more searchable, like **"Number Theory & Cryptography: Visual Math Lab."**
* **The "First 3 Screenshots":** Your current screenshots should highlight the most "wow" visualizers immediately (like the Ulam Spiral or Pascal's Triangle).
* **Encourage Reviews:** Reach out to your current 50+ users (friends/colleagues) and ask for honest 5-star reviews. The Play Store algorithm prioritizes apps with a high rating-to-download ratio.
* **Open Source or "Freemium" Logic:** Since it's currently a "Premium Design" with no ads, highlight that in the very first line of your description. "100% Ad-Free & Offline" is a major selling point.
